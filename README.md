# Hr-attrition-analysis
Excel analysis of the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset (1,470 employees), built using Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, and rate-based comparisons.

## Tools and Formulars Used
- Excel Pivot Tables & Pivot Charts
- COUNTIF / AVERAGEIF
- Rate vs. count comparison charts

## Key Findings

1. Overall Attrition Rate
16.1% of employees (237 out of 1,470) left the company.

2. OverTime Is Strongly Linked to Attrition
Employees working overtime leave at nearly 3x the rate of those who don't — 30.5% vs. 10.4% respectfully.

3. Department Attrition Rates
Sales department has the highest attrition rate (20.6%), followed by Human Resources (19.0%) and Research & Development (13.8%) — despite R&D having the highest raw count of departures (133 people), due to its much larger headcount.

4. Pay Is Associated With Attrition
Employees who left earned roughly 30% less on average ($4,787.09/month) than those who stayed ($6,832.74/month).
